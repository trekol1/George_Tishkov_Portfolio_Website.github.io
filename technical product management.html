Analysing large sets of temperature data
and implementing complex multi-component geothermal monitoring system from scratch

Introduction

I was working on the polar edge of Russia, on the peninsula Yamal.
The soil there is mostly frozen and never defrosts, which adds complexity to any construction work in this area and requires permanent monitoring of soil temperature beneath any construction, in order to avoid sliding.
The company TaisS which I was working for was doing geological surveys of permafrost soils and geothermal monitoring.

Initial challange

At the starting point I was analysing big datasets of soil temperature data and bulding visualizations in Excel.
The data was represented by periodical measurements of soil temperature inside particular borehole recorded on a specific depth. Over 7000 sensors were processed simultaniously.

Main product

Preconditions

The data for datasets was collected manually by specific employees of the company, which required significant human resources to be involved and encreared costs substantionally.
Moreover, collecting data manualy caused a delay in responce, which was critical especialy during the season change.

Product objective

The company needed to implement a hardware system to collect the data automatically, and post it to a remote database, in order to give service engineers instant access to the data.
My challenge was to manage implementation of this system from the idea stage to the launch and commissioning.

Work process

I started with collecting requirements for the system from CEO and internal engineers, and conducted an industry research to determine a list of possible contractors to manufacture the system.
After I presented a list of 4 possible contractors and the contractor was chosen, I was in charge of forming system requirements and specifications for the for the manufacturer.
The system got the alias of ASGM (automated system for geotechnical monitoring) and after it was manufactured and shiped I coordinated the installation and commissioning process.

Technical details

The system consists of thermocouples (which are chains of temperature sensors connected by a cable) located in thermometric boreholes, and a controller that combines all sensors and provides registration, accumulation and loading of data into a remote database.

The controller is equipped with:

  • GSM modem controller to transfer the data to the remote database
  • a non-volatile memory, to store collected data in case of connection loss
  • a redundant power supply, ensuring system operability in case of outage
Special software on a remote server processes data received from the controller and, in the event of an emergency violation of the temperature regime or failure of system elements sends an urgent notification.

Expanding the product with additional feature

There still was room for improvement

After the ASGM system was put into operation and large costs required constantly for making the measurements were reduced, the collected data still required manual processing and visualization.
At this point I puropsed the idea of developing a web-based feature for real-time visualization of the collected data.

Work process

I presented possible benefits that such feature could bring, and after the project was approved I designed prototype, formed requirements and specifications, brought in freelance contractors from among my old colleagues, and coordinated feature development and integration.

Technical details

The backend of the feature was implemented on PHP using Laravel framework. Voyager admin panel was used, and has been customized for project's specific needs.
Frontend was built using HTML, Javascript (mainly used jQuery). Visualizations were built using D3.js library, which is a pretty low level library. There are higher-level libraries, that solved problems similar to ours, but the project had a lot of specific requirements, and no one of discovered libraries fit them all.
Intuitive and simple user interface is available remotely just like a website, allows to view data and graphs and also to customise visualisations and adapt them for downloading as JPG/PDF and printing.

Results

After the project was finished, company had 3 major outcomes
  • Large companies resourses required constantly for manual collecting data were freed and could be directed to other activities.
  • System development and implementation costs were soon broke even due to reduced fixed costs.
  • Engineers got access to temperature data in real-time mode, which allowed them to instantly respond to any changes or emergencies.
